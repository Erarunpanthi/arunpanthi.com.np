Estimation and Valuation in Civil Engineering

What is Estimation?

Estimation in civil engineering is the process of calculating the approximate cost, quantities, and resources required for a construction project.

Estimated Cost vs. Actual Cost

Estimated Cost: The projected cost of a project based on preliminary calculations and assumptions.

Actual Cost: The final cost incurred after completion of the project, which may differ from the estimated cost due to various factors like price escalation, unforeseen circumstances, and design changes.

Types of Estimates

  • Preliminary Estimate: A rough estimate made in the early stages to assess project feasibility.
  • Detailed Estimate: A comprehensive estimate that includes itemized costs for materials, labor, and overheads.
  • Quantity Estimate: A method where the total quantity of materials required is determined before estimating the cost.
  • Revised Estimate: An estimate prepared when there are significant changes in the project scope or cost.
  • Supplementary Estimate: An additional estimate required due to modifications in the original design.
  • Annual Repair and Maintenance Estimate: A cost estimate prepared for periodic maintenance of structures.

Purpose of Estimation

  • To determine the approximate cost of the project.
  • To help in financial planning and budget allocation.
  • To assess material and labor requirements.
  • To avoid cost overruns and financial losses.
  • To compare different design alternatives based on cost-effectiveness.
