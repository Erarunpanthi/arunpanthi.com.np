Why This Page Matters for Lok Sewa Aspirants

Nepal's Public Service Commission (लोक सेवा आयोग) is the constitutional body responsible for selecting meritorious candidates for government civil service positions. Under Article 244 of the Constitution of Nepal, each of the 7 provinces maintains its own Pradesh Lok Sewa Aayog to recruit provincial and local-level government employees.

This page consolidates all 8 official Lok Sewa websites (1 Federal + 7 Provincial) along with their 8 online application form portals — giving you 16 direct, verified links with official logos to apply for government jobs across Nepal.

Understanding Nepal's Lok Sewa Aayog System

What is Lok Sewa Aayog?

The Lok Sewa Aayog (लोक सेवा आयोग), known in English as the Public Service Commission (PSC), is Nepal's independent constitutional body established on June 15, 1951 (Ashad 1, 2008 BS). Its primary function is to conduct competitive examinations and select qualified candidates for civil service appointments across all levels of the Government of Nepal.

Provincial Lok Sewa Aayog — A Constitutional Mandate

Following Nepal's adoption of the federal structure under the Constitution of Nepal 2072, Article 244 mandates that each of the seven provinces shall have its own Province Public Service Commission. These commissions recruit for provincial civil service, provincial police service, local government service, and organized institutions. The Province PSC Act 2076 further operationalized these bodies across all seven provinces.

How to Apply Online for Lok Sewa?

All Lok Sewa applications are now processed entirely online. For federal positions, candidates register through psconline.psc.gov.np. For provincial positions, each province has its own online portal. The process involves registering, completing your profile, selecting your desired vacancy, uploading documents, and making online payment through eSewa, ConnectIPS, or banks.

Important Note on Gandaki Online Portal

Please note that the Gandaki Pradesh online application portal uses a slightly different URL pattern: onlineppsc.gandaki.gov.np instead of the ppsconline pattern used by other provinces. Always verify the correct URL before applying.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

What is the official website of the Federal Lok Sewa Aayog?

The official website is psc.gov.np and its online form portal is psconline.psc.gov.np.

How many Provincial Lok Sewa Aayog are there in Nepal?

There are 7 — one for each province: Koshi, Madhesh, Bagmati, Gandaki, Lumbini, Karnali, and Sudurpashchim. Each operates independently under Article 244 of the Constitution.

Can I apply to both Federal and Provincial Lok Sewa simultaneously?

Yes, they are independent commissions with separate portals and exam schedules. However, if exam dates overlap, you'll need to choose one.

Why does Gandaki have a different online portal URL?

Gandaki Province uses onlineppsc.gandaki.gov.np instead of the ppsconline pattern. This is simply a different subdomain configuration by the province's IT department. The functionality remains the same.

What is the age limit for Lok Sewa examinations?

For most positions, the minimum age is 18 and maximum is 35. For female and disabled candidates, the upper limit is 40 years. No age limit for permanent civil servants currently in service.

Will my federal PSC account work on provincial portals?

No. Each portal requires separate registration. Your federal PSC login at psconline.psc.gov.np will not work on any provincial portal. You must create a new account on each province's system.
