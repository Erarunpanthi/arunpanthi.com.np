Structural Design (RCC Notes)

Cement

Cement is a fine material with adhesive and cohesive properties, used as a binding medium.

  • Types of Cement:
    • Ordinary Portland Cement (OPC)
    • Low Heat Portland Cement
    • Portland Pozzolana Cement (PPC)
    • High Alumina Cement

Concrete

Cement Concrete (PCC): A composite material made of coarse aggregate, fine aggregate, and cement.

Reinforced Cement Concrete (RCC): Steel + PCC.

Advantages of Concrete

  • Durable and resistant to atmospheric effects.
  • Fire-resistant and economical.
  • Can be molded into any shape and size.
  • Monolithic construction is possible.

Workability of Concrete

Ease of mixing, handling, and compacting.

Key Factor: Water content in the mix.

Durability of Concrete

Resistance to disintegration and decay.

Factors Affecting Durability:

  • Use of less durable aggregates.
  • Freezing and thawing of water.
  • Temperature changes (wetting and drying).
  • Excessive pores in concrete.

Grades of Concrete

IS 456:2000 specifies 15 grades of concrete (M10 to M80).

  • M refers to the mix, and the number indicates the characteristic strength in N/mm².
  • Minimum grade for RCC work: M20.

Water Cement Ratio (W/C Ratio)

W/C Ratio affects workability and strength.

  • Low W/C ratio: Reduces strength.
  • High W/C ratio: Increases workability but decreases strength.

Steel

Steel Grades: Fe 250, Fe 415, Fe 500, Fe 550.

  • Modulus of Elasticity (Es): 2 x 105 N/mm².
  • HYSD bars are used with M20 or higher-grade concrete.

Reinforced Cement Concrete Design

1. Working Stress Method (WSM)

  • Factor of Safety: 3 for concrete, 1.8 for steel.
  • Disadvantages: Uneconomical, does not show true safety factor.

2. Ultimate Load Method (ULM)

Analyzes stress at impending collapse.

3. Limit State Method (LSM)

Most widely used method.

  • Partial Safety Factors: 1.5 for concrete, 1.15 for steel.

Beam Design

Singly Reinforced Section

  • Reinforcement only in the tension zone.
  • Assumptions: Plane sections remain plane, maximum strain in concrete = 0.0035.

Doubly Reinforced Section

Used when depth is restricted or bending moment changes sign.

Slab Design

  • One-way Slab: Longer span/Shorter span > 2.
  • Two-way Slab: Longer span/Shorter span ≤ 2.

Column Design

  • Long Column: Effective length/Least lateral dimension > 12.
  • Short Column: Effective length/Least lateral dimension ≤ 12.

Footing Design

Foundation: Transmits load to the soil.

  • Minimum depth of foundation is determined by Rankine theory.
