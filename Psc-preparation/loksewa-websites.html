Why This Page Matters for Lok Sewa Aspirants

Nepal's Public Service Commission (लोक सेवा आयोग) was established on June 15, 1951, and serves as the constitutional body responsible for selecting meritorious candidates for government civil service positions. Under Article 244 of the Constitution of Nepal, each of the 7 provinces maintains its own Pradesh Lok Sewa Aayog (Province Public Service Commission) to recruit provincial and local-level government employees.

Navigating through multiple government portals can be confusing for aspirants. This page consolidates all 8 official Lok Sewa websites (1 Federal + 7 Provincial) along with their online application form portals — giving you direct, verified access to apply for government jobs across Nepal. Whether you're applying for a Kharidar, Nayab Subba, Section Officer, or any technical post, you'll find the right link here.

Understanding Nepal's Lok Sewa Aayog System

What is Lok Sewa Aayog?

The Lok Sewa Aayog (लोक सेवा आयोग), known in English as the Public Service Commission (PSC), is Nepal's independent constitutional body established on June 15, 1951 (Ashad 1, 2008 BS). Its primary function is to conduct competitive examinations and select qualified candidates for civil service appointments across all levels of the Government of Nepal. The Federal PSC, headquartered at Kamalpokhari, Kathmandu, handles recruitment for federal civil service, Nepal Army, Nepal Police, Armed Police Force, and other federal government services.

Provincial Lok Sewa Aayog — A Constitutional Mandate

Following Nepal's adoption of the federal structure under the Constitution of Nepal 2072, Article 244 mandates that each of the seven provinces shall have its own Province Public Service Commission (प्रदेश लोक सेवा आयोग). These provincial commissions are responsible for recruiting employees for provincial civil service, provincial police service, local government service, and provincial organized institutions. The Province PSC Act 2076 further operationalized these bodies across all seven provinces.

How to Apply Online for Lok Sewa?

All Lok Sewa applications — both federal and provincial — are now processed entirely online. For federal positions, candidates register and apply through psconline.psc.gov.np. For provincial positions, each province has its own online recruitment management system. The process involves registering with personal details, completing your profile, selecting your desired vacancy, uploading required documents, and making online payment through banks like Global IME Bank, Rastriya Banijya Bank, or ConnectIPS e-payment.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

What is the official website of the Federal Lok Sewa Aayog?

The official website of the Federal Lok Sewa Aayog (Public Service Commission Nepal) is psc.gov.np and its online application portal is psconline.psc.gov.np.

How many Provincial Lok Sewa Aayog are there in Nepal?

There are 7 Provincial Lok Sewa Aayog in Nepal — one for each province: Koshi, Madhesh, Bagmati, Gandaki, Lumbini, Karnali, and Sudurpashchim. Each operates as an independent constitutional body under Article 244 of the Constitution of Nepal.

Can I apply to both Federal and Provincial Lok Sewa at the same time?

Yes, you can apply for both federal and provincial Lok Sewa vacancies simultaneously, as they are independent commissions with separate application portals and examination schedules. However, if exam dates overlap, you will need to choose one.

What is the age limit for Lok Sewa examinations?

For most positions like Kharidar and Nayab Subba, the minimum age is 18 and maximum is 35. For female and physically disabled candidates, the upper age limit is extended to 40 years. There is no age limit for permanent civil servants currently in service.

Are all provincial Lok Sewa online portals the same in design?

Yes, all provincial Lok Sewa online application portals follow the same design and user interface for the convenience of applicants. Once you learn to use one portal, you can apply through any province's system using the same procedures.
