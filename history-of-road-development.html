History of Road Development in Nepal

Introduction

  • The history of road development in Nepal is relatively short.
  • The first motorable road was constructed in the Kathmandu Valley in 1924.
  • By the 1950s, Nepal had only 376 km of roads.
  • As of today, Nepal's total road length stands at 18,828 km, including district and urban roads.

Government Initiatives

  • The Government of Nepal (GON) has prioritized road development since the beginning of planned development in 1956.
  • In 1953, the government began linking Kathmandu to the southern border with Indian assistance, opening it for traffic by 1956.
  • In 1966, the Government of China constructed the Kathmandu–Kodari road, connecting Nepal to the northern border.
  • The East-West Highway (1,028 km) was initiated during this period with support from bilateral and multilateral agencies.
  • The Strategic Road Network (SRN) includes national highways and feeder roads, forming the backbone of Nepal's road infrastructure.
  • The current SRN covers 7,917 km with plans to extend it to 9,900 km by 2016.

Key Agencies Involved in Road Development

  • Department of Roads (DoR): The central agency for road construction and maintenance.
  • DoLIDAR: Responsible for rural infrastructure development, especially feeder roads.
  • Municipalities: Local entities managing urban road projects and maintenance.

Historical Background of Roads in Nepal

Three Key Periods of Road Development

  • Before 2007 B.S. (Rana Era): The construction of roads was limited to the Kathmandu Valley with minimal infrastructure.
  • 2007 B.S. to 2027 B.S. (Post-Rana Era): The establishment of the Public Works Department (PWD) and the completion of significant roads like the Tribhuvan Rajpath and Kanti Rajpath.
  • After 2027 B.S. (Modern Era): Road development strategy implemented with focus on National Highways, Feeder Roads, and Strategic Road Networks.

Significant Road Projects

  • Tribhuvan Rajpath (2013 B.S.) - The first motorable road connecting Kathmandu to Bhainse built with Indian army engineers.
  • East-West Highway - A major national highway covering the full length of Nepal from east to west, initiated in 2019 B.S..
  • Hetauda-Narayangadh Road - A key all-weather, two-lane road completed in 2015 B.S..
  • Kodari Highway - Connecting Kathmandu to China, providing a significant trade route between Nepal and Tibet.

Present Road Infrastructure

  • The National Road Network now comprises 18,828 km of roads, including 4,977 km of national highways and district roads.
  • Out of the 77 districts, 76 are connected to district headquarters by national road network (except Humla).
  • 72 districts are now accessible by blacktopped roads.
  • The total length of national highways is approximately 14,913 km, with around 7,000 km of blacktopped roads.

Urban Road Development

  • Urban roads provide access to residential, business, and industrial areas within city limits.
  • As of 2004, DOR reported a total of 2,259.9 km of urban roads in Nepal.
  • The Kathmandu Valley had just 325 km of motorable roads in 1992, with 150 km in poor condition.

Future Plans

  • The construction of the Outer Ring Road (ORR), a proposed 72 km long route around Kathmandu to handle future traffic demands.
  • Expansion of the Inner Ring Road, supported by Chinese aid and a proposed 8-lane extension.
  • Plans to connect the remaining district headquarters by road, with Chame (Manang), Dunai (Dolpa), Simikot (Humla), and Gamgadhi (Mugu) being the last few unconnected districts.

Ongoing Projects

  • The Mid-Hill Highway project, aimed at improving connectivity in the mid-hills of Nepal.
  • Widening of Koteshwor-Bhaktapur (6-lane) sector of Araniko Highway with JICA funding.
  • Extension of North-South Corridor for improved trade routes.
